# Shot Types Reference

Five canonical shot types for musical/MV projects. Each type is defined by its visual requirements, lip-sync needs, and best use cases across multiple genres.

---

## `lip_sync_closeup`

**Definition:** Face is clearly visible, stable, and roughly frontal. The viewer must believe the character is singing the exact words. This is a Wav2Lip candidate — the face stays readable throughout for later lip-sync correction.

**When to use:**
- Emotionally important lyric phrases that land with impact
- Hook lines / earworm moments where the viewer expects to see the singer
- Dialogue-to-song transitions in musical theater
- The climax phrase of a ballad or the title drop

**Visual requirements:**
- Face fills at least 40% of the frame
- Angle: frontal to 3/4, never full profile or back of head
- Camera: static or slow push-in (≤ 0.5× zoom over shot duration)
- Mouth must be visible and not obscured by motion blur, hands, or objects
- Stable head position — no whip pans or heavy shake
- Lighting on face must be even and clear

**Genre examples:**

| Genre | Example |
|-------|---------|
| Pop ballad | Emotional climax — singer's face fills frame, tears on cheek, singing "and I'll never let you go", warm golden light |
| Chinese 唱跳 | Chorus hook line — lead singer center frame, backup dancers blurred behind, singing "你是我心中不灭的火", LED stage backdrop |
| Musical theater | Dialogue-to-song moment — character steps forward from ensemble, face lit by single spotlight, begins singing first phrase |
| EDM (with vocal) | Vocal chop close-up — brief 2s face shot during the lone vocal phrase before the drop |
| R&B / soul | Intimate confession — tight close-up, shallow depth of field, singer almost whispering into camera |

## `performance_medium`

**Definition:** Singer is visible from roughly waist up. They are performing, singing, and moving — but exact mouth sync is less critical. The viewer gets the *feel* of performance, not a word-by-word read.

**When to use:**
- Verse sections where the singer moves through space
- Pre-chorus build-ups with escalating gestures
- Bridge sections with emotional intensity but broader physical expression
- Any section where the character's body language tells the story alongside the vocal

**Visual requirements:**
- Body visible from roughly waist up (medium shot)
- Angle: side, 3/4, or walking toward/away from camera — more freedom than closeup
- Camera: can track, dolly, or have gentle movement
- Rough mouth motion is acceptable — the face doesn't need to be Wav2Lip-quality
- Environment should be visible and contribute to mood
- Props, costumes, and staging are visible and meaningful

**Genre examples:**

| Genre | Example |
|-------|---------|
| Pop | Verse walk-and-sing — singer strolling down a city sidewalk at golden hour, casual hand gestures, singing softly |
| Chinese 唱跳 | Pre-chorus gesture build — singer half-turns toward camera with arms spreading wide, backup dancers in periphery, rising energy |
| Ballad | Bridge emotional peak — singer standing at ocean cliff edge, wind in hair, arms raised, belting high note, camera slowly pulling back |
| Musical theater | "I Want" song verse — character moving through their world (kitchen, classroom, street), interacting with objects while singing |
| EDM | DJ/performance shot — performer at booth, head bobbing, raising hands to crowd, laser lights sweeping behind |

## `dance_or_group`

**Definition:** Choreography, crowd dynamics, ensemble movement, or ritual action drives the shot. The music moves *bodies*, not mouths. No lip sync required or expected.

**When to use:**
- Chorus dance breaks in pop/唱跳
- EDM drops where physical energy peaks
- Musical theater ensemble numbers
- Any section where the collective motion is the visual story
- Concert / live performance wide shots

**Visual requirements:**
- Multiple bodies visible (2+ dancers, crowd, ensemble)
- Camera: wide to medium-wide, can be dynamic — low angle, crane, tracking
- Movement is the star — choreography patterns, formations, synchronization
- Energy level should match the music (staccato for fast beats, flowing for slow)
- Mouth sync is irrelevant — faces may be visible but lip match is not expected
- Group dynamics matter: formations, spacing, synchronization, contrast

**Genre examples:**

| Genre | Example |
|-------|---------|
| Pop/唱跳 | Chorus dance break — 6 dancers in tight formation, sharp synchronized moves on each beat, LED floor panels, low-angle wide shot |
| EDM | Drop section — massive crowd shot from stage, hands in the air, strobe lights, bass shakes the frame, confetti cannons |
| Chinese 舞蹈 | 舞蹈 solo — single dancer in traditional costume, fluid ribbon work, slow-motion capture, studio with seamless backdrop |
| Musical theater | Group number — full ensemble on stage, tiered staging, kick-line or tableau, spotlights sweeping |
| K-pop | Post-chorus point choreography — tight formation dancing, each member hits signature move, camera circles the group |

## `mv_broll`

**Definition:** Symbolic, atmospheric, or cinematic visuals that carry mood and meaning without any singing. No mouth sync. These shots are the visual poetry of the MV.

**When to use:**
- Intro / outro establishment
- Instrumental interludes
- Lyric metaphors made visual (fire = passion, rain = sadness, etc.)
- Pacing relief between performance shots
- World-building and visual theme establishment
- Visual foreshadowing or callback

**Visual requirements:**
- No human face that needs to sync
- Atmosphere is primary: color, texture, light, weather, landscape
- Can include symbolic objects, architecture, nature, weather, cityscapes
- Camera movement should match mood (slow drift for contemplative, aerial sweep for epic)
- Works as visual punctuation — gives the viewer's eye a rest
- Strong composition and color palette are essential

**Genre examples:**

| Genre | Example |
|-------|---------|
| Ballad | Rain on window, slow slide down glass droplets, grey-blue palette, interior warm light visible through blur |
| Chinese 唱跳 | Empty neon-lit stage before the performer enters, colored smoke curling, camera slowly panning across |
| EDM | Aerial city flyover at night, headlights streaming like blood vessels, pulsing building lights synced to bass |
| Musical theater | The empty theater seat from which the protagonist will later sing, dust particles in spotlight beam |
| Pop | Single red rose falling in slow motion onto black water surface, ripples expanding, high-speed macro |

## `transition`

**Definition:** Short visual bridges (typically 0.5–2s) that connect shots or mark section changes. These are punctuation marks in the visual language of the MV.

**When to use:**
- Between major sections (verse → chorus, chorus → bridge)
- To mark a tonal shift (quiet → loud, inside → outside)
- As visual bookends for a repeated motif
- To smooth jarring cuts between very different shot types
- As rhythmic accents on strong beats

**Visual requirements:**
- Duration: 0.5–2 seconds maximum
- Motion-based: the shot IS the movement
- Should feel intentional, not like a lazy crossfade
- Can be abstract: light flare, shadow wipe, color wash
- Can be concrete: whip pan through door, match cut on object, silhouette transition
- No lip sync — these are pure visual devices

**Genre examples:**

| Genre | Example |
|-------|---------|
| Pop | Whip pan from dark backstage through curtain into blazing stage lights |
| Chinese 唱跳 | Smoke wipe — dense colored smoke fills frame then clears to reveal new scene |
| Ballad | Slow dissolve from rain-streaked window to dry window with morning light |
| EDM | Flash/strobe cut — white flash on beat, new scene revealed in the afterimage |
| Musical theater | Silhouette transition — character's silhouette grows to fill frame, becomes shadow of next set |
| All genres | Object match cut — close-up of falling rose petal → close-up of red dress fabric swirling |

## Shot Type Selection Quick Guide

```
Is there a clear solo vocal with important lyrics?
  ├── YES: Is this the emotional/structural peak?
  │    ├── YES → lip_sync_closeup
  │    └── NO  → performance_medium
  └── NO: Is there choreography, crowd, or ensemble movement?
       ├── YES → dance_or_group
       └── NO: Is this between 0.5–2s and connecting sections?
            ├── YES → transition
            └── NO  → mv_broll
```

**Note:** This is a starting heuristic. The final decision should also consider:
- Vocal density from `audio_analysis.json` (see `lip-sync-policy.md`)
- Energy level and its trajectory (rising, peaking, falling)
- Position in the song's emotional arc
- Visual variety — avoid five consecutive shots of the same type
